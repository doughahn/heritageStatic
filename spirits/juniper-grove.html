This gin begins with a neutral spirit. We distill it like Source One, and then redistill it a third time over botanicals: juniper, lime, lemon, coriander, and angelica. We put botanicals into the pot the day of distillation, while running a higher ABV, to achieve bold flavors and big extractions for this gin. Finally, we proof with demineralized water. This technique produces a juniper-forward, citrusy gin that is perfect for simple drinks like the classic G&T and bold enough to hold up in negronis.

Atrium Gin

A contemporary estate gin distilled from grains grown on on land in Nevada.

  • Produced from ten different botanicals.
  • Produced in small batches and finished in copper pot stills.
  • Triple-distilled.

Tasting Notes

Bright notes highlighted by grapefruit citrus hit the front of the palate, developing into a complex bouquet. This is a tremendously flower-forward gin that captures a spectrum of experiences ranging from lavender to spice.

750 ml; 46% ALC/VOL

The juniper is evident but not overpowering, and for me there was a strong orange warmth to the aroma, along with spiciness from the cardamom, and big floral notes from the gentian, lavender, and chamomile.

— Travel Distilled

The botanicals in this gin are juniper, angelica, gentian, lavender, chamomile, orange peel, orris root, cardamom, grapefruit, and lemon balm. Atrium begins with a neutral spirit that we distill like Source One and then macerate the botanicals overnight before distilling again, which pulls a compelling flavor out. The name Atrium is a reference to the greenhouse where we hope to grow our own variants of local botanicals someday soon.
